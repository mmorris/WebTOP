Fresnel Diffraction, Circular Aperture: Theory
Off-axis Intensity

Let us now consider off-axis (r¹ 0) observation points in the region to the right of the aperture. Such points lie in one of three different regions: (a) the "lit" region,0<r<R, (b) the "shadow" region, r>R, or (c) the shadow boundary region, r=R. The formula we will use to evaluate the intensity at various points will be different in these different regions.

Figures 4 –6 below show the intensity patterns obtained when the Fresnel numbers are, respectively, five, six, and seven. In each figure the top part displays a plot of the intensity as a function of position along the x-axis in the observation plane. The bottom portion of each figure shows a WebTOP visualization of what the pattern on the screen will look like. Note that in Fig. 4 and Fig. 5 the Fresnel numbers are both odd, and the on axis intensity is high [as was predicted by (a) above]. In Fig. 6, the Fresnel number is even, and the intensity on-axis is zero. When we look at the intensity patterns in these three cases, we also see a "trend" which turns out to be quite general, namely the number of "major" maxima observed across the intensity pattern is equal to the Fresnel number.
