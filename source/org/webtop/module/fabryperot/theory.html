Interference - Fabry-Perot Etalon: Theory

Introduction

Consider light from a monochromatic point source of wavelength l that passes through a large circular aperture (so large that diffraction effects are negligible) and is incident upon a Fabry-Perot Etalon. (The role of the aperture is that it insures that only a finite cone of rays is incident upon the etalon.) The etalon is a slab of dielectric material of index of refraction n and thickness d. The slab is surrounded by air. Both faces of the slab are coated with the same material and have reflectance R. The light that transmits through the etalon is incident upon an observation screen. This module depicts the resulting intensity pattern on the observation screen, and displays a plot of resulting intensity as a function of position across the center of the intensity pattern. See Fig. 1.  For convenience, the circular aperture is not pictured in Fig. 1.
