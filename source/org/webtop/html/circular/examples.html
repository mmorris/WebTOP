Diffraction - Fresnel Circular Aperture and Obstacle: Examples

To view an example, press the button at the end of the description. Once the module finishes loading, press the play button at the recording panel. If you want to save the example code to your computer and run it locally right click of the disk icon and save the file to disk. Use wsl as an extension of the file you save to your computer.

Example 1 Changing the Diameter of the Aperture: Even Fresnel Numbers

In this example a plane wave of light of wavelength 500 nm is normally incident upon a circular aperture, and the resulting intensity pattern is viewed on an observation screen 50 mm from the aperture. In this simulation, the diameter of the aperture is varied; diameters that correspond to Fresnel numbers of 10, 8, 6, and 4 are used, consecutively.  In each case, is the on-axis intensity a maximum or a minimum?  Starting at one edge of the intensity pattern and proceeding across it through the origin to the other edge, how many “major” maxima do you encounter?

Example 2 Changing the Wavelength of the Light: Even and Odd Fresnel Numbers

In this example a monochromatic plane wave of light is normally incident upon a circular aperture of diameter 1.00 mm, and the resulting intensity pattern is viewed on an observation screen 50 mm from the aperture. In this simulation, the wavelength of the incident light varies. Wavelengths that correspond to Fresnel numbers of 12, 11, 10 and 9 are used, consecutively. In each case, is the on-axis intensity a maximum or a minimum?  Starting at one edge of the intensity pattern and proceeding across it through the origin to the other edge, how many “major” maxima do you encounter?

Example 3 Changing  the Distance from the Aperture Plane to the Observation Plane: Even Fresnel Numbers

In this example a plane wave of light of wavelength 500 nm is normally incident upon a circular aperture of diameter 1.00 mm, and the resulting intensity pattern is viewed on an observation screen. In this simulation, the distance z from the aperture to the observation screen is varied.  Values of z that produce Fresnel numbers of 10, 8, 6 and 4 are used, consecutively. In each case, is the on-axis intensity a maximum or a minimum?  Starting at one edge of the intensity pattern and proceeding across it through the origin to the other edge, how many “major” maxima do you encounter?
