Scattering: Examples

To view an example, press the button at the end of the description. Once the module finishes loading, press the play button at the recording panel. If you want to save the example code to your computer and run it locally right click of the disk icon and save the file to disk. Use wsl as an extension of the file you save to your computer.

Example 1 Polarization of Scattered Field – Linearly Polarized Incident Field

In this example a monochromatic plane wave of light linearly polarized in the z-direction and traveling in the positive y-direction is incident upon an atom located at the origin. The electric field of the scattered light is depicted in three different directions: the positive x-direction, the positive z-direction, and a direction determined by the position of the movable observation screen. In this example two different situations are investigated. In both cases, the wavelength of the light is 500 nm, E0x = 0, and E0z =1.

(a) The movable observation screen begins at the position q = 90 degrees, f =180 degrees. A large set of observation points in the x-y plane are explored by varying the azimuthal angle f from 180 to 0 degrees, and then back to 180 degrees again. The polarization of the light is the same at all the observation points.

(b) The movable observation screen begins at the position q = 90 degrees, f = 180 degrees. A large set of observation points in the x-z plane are explored by varying the polar angle q from 90 to 0 degrees, and then back to 90 degrees again. The polarization of the light is not the same at these observation points.

Example 2 Polarization of Scattered Field – Unpolarized Incident Field

In this example a unpolarized plane wave of light traveling in the positive y-direction is incident upon an atom located at the origin, and the electric field of the scattered light is simulated. The movable observation screen starts at the position q = 90 degrees, f =180 degrees; the scattered light there is linearly polarized. The movable observation screen is then moved to the position q = 90 degrees, f =90 degrees; the scattered light there is unpolarized. Finally, the movable observation screen is moved to the position q = 90 degrees, f =11 degrees; the scattered light there is partially polarized.

Example 3 Wavelength Dependence of the Scattered Light – Linearly Polarized Light Incident

In this example a monochromatic plane wave of linearly polarized light traveling in the positive y-direction and linearly polarized in the z-direction is incident upon an atom located at the origin, and the electric field of the scattered light is simulated. The following two cases are simulated, and then repeated: (a) wavelength = 400 nm, (b) wavelength = 700 nm. The scattered field is much stronger in the former case.
