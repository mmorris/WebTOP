Exercise 1:  Scattering of Linearly Polarized Light - Polarization of the Scattered Field at Observation Points in the Plane Perpendicular to the Incident Electric Field

Choose the default parameter values, except set E0x = 0 and f = 180 degrees. At observation points along the positive and negative x-axes the scattered electric field is linearly polarized in the z-direction. Use the Phi keyboard entry box  to determine the direction of the polarization of the scattered electric field in the following directions. In each case you may want to use the Rotate icon (the checkered ball) to rotate the scene about the z-axis a little, in order to get a better view.

(a) q = 90 degrees, f = 135 degrees,

(b) q = 90 degrees, f = 90 degrees,

(c) q = 90 degrees, f = 45 degrees.

Exercise 2:  Scattering of Linearly Polarized Light - Polarization of the Scattered Field at Observation Points in a Plane that Contains the Incident Electric Field

Choose the default parameter values, except set E0x = 0 and f = 180 degrees. At observation points along the positive and negative x-axes the scattered electric field is linearly polarized in the z-direction. The intensity of the light at observation points along the positive z-direction is zero.

Use the Rotate icon to rotate the scene about the z-axis until the positive y-direction is pointed out of your computer screen. Grab the blue cube on the observation screen and slowly drag the movable observation screen towards the positive z-axis. Does the intensity of the light increase or decrease, as the screen gets closer to the z-axis?

Exercise 3:  Scattering of Linearly Polarized Light - Wavelength Dependence of the Scattered Electric Field

Choose the default parameter values, except set E0x = 0 and f = 180 degrees. Use the Rotate icon to rotate the scene about the z-axis until the positive y-direction is pointed out of your computer screen. The current value of the wavelength is 400 nm. Keep your eye on the electric field vectors along the positive or negative x-direction, and change the wavelength of the incident light to 700 nm. Does this long wavelength light scatter more strongly or less strongly than the shorter 400 nm light?

Exercise 4:  Scattering of Unpolarized Light - Polarization

Choose the default parameter values, except click on the Unpolarized tab, and set f = 90 degrees.

(a) The scattered electric field vectors in the positive x-direction and the positive z-direction are linearly polarized, but in different directions. What are these directions?

(b) The scattered light in the forward direction is unpolarized. Set f = 15 degrees. The scattered electric field vector in the direction q = 90 degrees, f = 15 degrees is neither completely polarized, nor completely unpolarized; it is referred to as partially polarized light. Describe, in words, the behavior of the electric field vector in the observation plane perpendicular to this direction.
