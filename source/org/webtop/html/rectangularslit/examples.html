Rectangular Aperture: Examples

To view an example, press the button at the end of the description. Once the module finishes loading, press the play button at the recording panel. If you want to save the example code to your computer and run it locally right click of the disk icon and save the file to disk. Use wsl as an extension of the file you save to your computer.

Example 1 Varying the Wavelength of the Incident Light

In this example the initial wavelength of the incident light is 400 nm. The wavelength is then slowly increased to 700 nm, and then decreased back to 400 nm. Note that as the wavelength increases (decreases) the amount of diffraction increases (decreases) .

 

Example 2  Varying the Widths of the Aperture

In this example, the aperture starts out square; its width is 0.20 mm in each direction. The width in the x-direction is then increased until its value is 0.70 mm. The width in the y-direction is then increased until its value is 0.70 mm. Finally the widths are decreased simulataneously until they reach their original values.

Example 3 Varying the Distance to the Observation Screen

In this example the distance, z, from the aperture plane to the observation plane is initially 1000 mm. The distance is then inceased to 2000 mm, and then decreased back to 1000 mm. This sequence of events is then repeated. Note that as z increases (decreases) the pattern expands outward (inward).
