Example 1 Number of Transverse Oscillations and the Mode of Observation

This example shows the relationship of the transverse oscillations in the waveguide with the mode number, m, with animation of the lowest and highest order modes. As the mode number increases from 0. .M, the bounce angle, θm increases, which causes an increase in the number of transverse oscillations. Note that the mode of observation, which is displayed on the console, may also be determined visually by counting the number of nodes (zeros) of the traveling wave.
(a) m = 0 with 1 transverse oscillation and 0 amplitude nodes.
(b) m = 1 with 2 transverse oscillations and 1 amplitude node.
(c) m = 2 with 3 transverse oscillations and 2 amplitude nodes.
(d) m = 3 with 4 transverse oscillations and 3 amplitude nodes.

Example 2 Effect of Changing the Width on the Total Number of Modes, M

This example shows the relationship between the width of the waveguide, d and the total number of modes, M, as presented in equation 1 of the theory. As the width of the waveguide increases, the number of total possible modes also increases. Five values of the width and the highest possible mode for each width are presented:
(a) d = 0.5μm, m = 0, M = 1
(b) d = 1.0μm, m = 0, m = 1, M = 2
(c) d = 2.0μm, m = 1, m = 2, m = 3, M = 4
(d) d = 3.0μm, m = 3, m = 4, m = 5, M = 6
(e) d = 4.0μm, m = 5, m = 6, m = 7, M = 8

Case (a) presents a single-mode waveguide while all other cases depict multimode waveguides.

Example 3 Evanescent Wave Decay Rate Dependence on the Wavelength

This example shows the effect of wavelength changes on the evanescent wave decay rate. As the wavelength increases, the decay rate of the evanescent wave decreases, causing the penetration depth of the wave to increase. The wavelength begins at 0.8μm and increases by 0.1μm to 1.5μm.

Example 4 Evanescent Wave Penetration Depth Dependence on m.

This example illustrates the relationship between the mode number, M, and the penetration depth of the evanescent wave in medium 2. The penetration depth is the distance in the x-direction required for the amplitude of the wave in medium 2 to decay to zero. Note that as the mode number increases, the penetration depth of the evanescent wave also increases.
