Exercise 1:  Constructive and destructive interference

This exercise allows the user to develop a better understanding of waveguides by observing the various viewpoints of light propagating in the waveguide.

(a) Using the default parameters, click the Play button.

(b) Click the third Viewpoint Icon to observe an animated side view. Stop the animation and observe the wave. The side view illustrates constructive and destructive interference of the plane waves that travel in the waveguide. Can you the peaks and troughs that result from constructive and destructive interference?

(c) Press the Center Viewpoint Icon for a front view. What characteristic of a consecutive wave follows the previous wave: a peak or a trough? Peaks and trough are aligned in what direction?

(d) Click the first viewpoint icon to return to the default view and parameters.

Exercise 2:  Varying the Index of Refraction of Medium 1

Use the default values for all parameters except n1. Remember, one condition of light propagation by total internal reflection is n1 > n2.

b)Increase n1 to 2.5. What is the increase in the number of modes and the number of transverse oscillations?

c)Decrease n1 to 1.35. Do any parameters change? Why or Why not?

d)Click the first viewpoint icon to return to the default view and parameters.

Exercise 4:  Wavelength Variation

The wavelength of propagation depends on the wavelength of the input light and n1.Increasing the value of the input wavelength with n1 held constant, effects the number of possible modes in the waveguide and the amount of TE and evanescent waves in each region of the waveguide.

a)Using the wavelength widget increase the wavelength to 1.3μm. What parameter on the console changes? Is the change an increase or decrease? How does the scene change in terms of the size and number of oscillations?

b)Continue to increase the wavelength to the maximum input value. What is this value?

c)Click the first viewpoint icon to return to the default view and parameters.
