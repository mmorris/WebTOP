Photoelectric Effect: Examples

To view an example, press the button at the end of the description. Once the module finishes loading, press the play button at the recording panel. If you want to save the example code to your computer and run it locally right click of the disk icon and save the file to disk. Use wsl as an extension of the file you save to your computer.

Example 1 Varying the Intensity of the Incident Light

In this example the intensity of the incident light begins at a very low value, 1.0 (arbitrary units), and very few photoelectrons are ejected. The intensity value is then increased to 10.0, and approximately ten times as many photoelectrons are ejected. The intensity is then decreased to 1.0, and then increased back to 10.0.

 

Example 2  Varying the Voltage Across the plates

The plate on the left is the positively charged anode, and the plate on the right is the negatively charged cathode. In this example photoelectrons are ejected from the anode with a kinetic energy of 3.70 eV. Initially the voltage across the plates is zero (i.e. they are uncharged), and the photoelectrons travel from the anode to the cathode at a constant velocity. The voltage is then changed to 3.00 volts, and the photoelectrons decelerate as they travel. The voltage is then changed to 3.60 volts, and then to 3.70 volts, and the photoelectrons decelerate even more. In the latter case the photoelectrons are stopped before they reach the cathode and return to the anode.

Example 3 Varying the Wavelength of the Incident Light

In this example the wavelength of the incident light begins at the value 200 nm, and is then increased to 400, 500, 600, and 625 nm. As the wavelength of the light is increased up to 600 nm, the kinetic energy of the ejected photoelectrons decreases. When the wavelength is 625 nm, the energy of each incident photon is less than the work function of the metal, and no photoelectrons are ejected.
