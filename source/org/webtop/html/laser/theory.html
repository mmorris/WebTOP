Lasers: Theory

Introduction

This module simulates the beam from a stable laser resonator oscillating in a TEM00, TEM10, TEM01, or TEM11 transverse mode. The resulting intensity pattern is displayed on an observation screen, and a graph of the intensity across the middle of the pattern is shown on the top of the observation screen (see Fig. 1). The appearance of the beam inside the resonator is also simulated. (Comment: By modes, we mean the “bare cavity” modes that the resonator has without any gain medium inside it. The actual modes are almost always very similar to the bare cavity modes.)

Figure 1 The Lasers module depicting the TEM00 transverse mode of a laser.

A schematic diagram of the setup is shown in Fig. 2 below. The cavity consists of two spherical mirrors, M1 and M2, of radii of curvature R1 and R2, respectively. The length of the cavity, i.e., the distance between the mirrors is L. The distance from M1 to the beam waist is L1. The location of the beam waist (the plane where the beam radius is minimum) is defined as the plane z = 0, and the optical axis of the resonator coincides with the z-axis. The spot size at the beam waist is w0. The distance from the beam waist to the observation screen is z, and the spot size on the observation screen is w(z). The distance from M1 to the observation screen is s = L1 + z.

Figure 2 Schematic diagram of the Lasers module.

Let l denote the wavelength of the light being generated by the laser. The current values of l, R1, R2, L, and s are displayed on the top line of the console of the module (see Fig. 1). The user can vary these parameters. The current values of the quantities w0, zR, L1, z, w(z), and the intensity at the center of the pattern are displayed on the third line of the console. Here zR is the Rayleigh length of the beam; it will be discussed in the following section.

The Beam Width Condition

If the spot size of the beam incident upon M1 or M2 is larger than the radius of the mirror, no beam will be displayed. The spot sizes on the two mirrors are, respectively, w1 º w(-L1) and w2 º w(L-L1), where w(z) is given by Eq. (8). The radius of each mirror is 1.27 mm.
