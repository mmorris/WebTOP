Reflection and Refraction - Vectorial: Theory

Introduction

Consider two homogeneous dielectric media that have a planar interface. The "incident" medium occupies the half space y > 0 and has index of refraction ni . The "transmitting" medium occupies the half space y < 0 and has index of refraction nt . This module simulates the reflection and refraction of a monochromatic electromagnetic plane wave from such an interface (see Fig. 1). For simplicity only the electric field (and not the magnetic field) is considered. The module also allows the incident field to be unpolarized, but the relevant theory for that case will not be treated here.

Brewster's Angle and the Critical Angle

For the component of the electric field that is parallel to the plane of incidence, there is a an angle called Brewster's angle, denoted by qB, that has special significance. When the angle of incidence is equal to qB , the component of the reflected electric field that is parallel to the plane of incidence is zero. As a result, whenever light is incident at the angle qB , the reflected light is linearly polarized, no matter what the state of polarization of the incident light. For the case of internal reflection (ni>nt) the critical angle qC is extremely important. If the angle of incidence is greater than the critical angle, the electric field in the transmitting medium is an evanescent wave, not a traveling wave. As a result no energy is transmitted through the interface; all the energy goes into the reflected field. In this case we say that total internal reflection occurs.
