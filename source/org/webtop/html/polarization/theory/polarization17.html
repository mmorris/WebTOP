4. Retarders

In a retarder, one of the two components of the incident electric field vector is caused to lag the other by a predetermined amount. In order to do this the light is sent normally incident upon a doubly refracting slab of material with faces parallel to the optical axis (OA) of the slab.

The way to establish the phase difference between the components is as follows. Consider the birefringent slab in Fig. 24, whose optical axis (OA) is the y-direction.

The index of refraction for the x-component of the field is no and this component travels through the medium at speed vperp [see Eq. (3.2)].
