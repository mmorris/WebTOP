3. Birefringence
3.1 Basic Physics

A birefringent material is a material that has two different indices of refraction. To see how this comes about, let us consider calcite, CaCO3. Figure 23 shows the arrangement of atoms in calcite. The carbon atom and the three oxygen atoms of each molecule all lie in the same plane (the horizontal plane in the figure); the calcium atoms do not lie in this plane, they are displaced vertically from it. Accordingly, when an electric field vector oriented parallel to the carbon-oxygen plane is incident upon the crystal, the crystal responds one way and when an electric field vector oriented perpendicular to this plane is incident upon the crystal, the crystal responds in a different way.
