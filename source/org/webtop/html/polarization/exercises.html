Exercise 1:  Various Kinds of Polarized Light and Unpolarized Light

(a) Choose the Polarized option by clicking on its tab. Set the values of the x- and y-components of the field to 1.0, and phase difference to 90 degrees. What kind of polarized light is this? Linearly polarized? Left circularly polarized? Right circularly polarized? Elliptically polarized?

(b) Change the x-component value to 0.5. What kind of polarized light is this? Linearly polarized? Left circularly polarized? Right circularly polarized? Elliptically polarized?

(c) Change the value of the phase difference between the x and y components to be 0. What kind of polarized light is this? Linearly polarized? Left circularly polarized? Right circularly polarized? Elliptically polarized?

(d) Click on the Unpolarized tab in the console. Comment on the pattern formed by the dots in the input plane.

 

Exercise 2:  Linear Polarizer – Linearly Polarized Light Incident

Choose the Polarized option for the incident electric field. Set the x-component of the incident electric field to 0.500, the y-component to 0.866, and the phase difference between the components to be 0. This light is linearly polarized at 60 degrees with respect to the positive x-axis. The intensity of the light is 1.00 (arbitrary units).

(a) Insert a polarizer and position it at z =10. Set the value of the Angle variable to zero. What is the polarization of the light exiting the polarizer? Calculate the intensity of the light exiting the polarizer and verify that its value agrees with WebTOP's result.

(b) Change the angle that the transmission axis makes with the positive x-axis to 45 degrees. What is the polarization of the light exiting the polarizer? What is the polarization of the light exiting the polarizer? Calculate the intensity of the light exiting the polarizer and verify that its value agrees with WebTOP's result.

(c) Change the angle that the transmission axis makes with the positive x-axis to 90 degrees. What is the polarization of the light exiting the polarizer? Calculate the intensity of the light exiting the polarizer and verify that its value agrees with WebTOP's result.

Exercise 5:  Linear Polarizer – Infamous Three-Polarizer Problem

Choose the Unpolarized option for the incident electric field. The intensity of the light is 1.00 (arbitrary units.)

(a) Add a polarizer and place it at the position z = 5. Set the value of the Angle variable to zero. What is the polarization of the light exiting the polarizer? Calculate the intensity of the light exiting the polarizer and verify that its value agrees with WebTOP's result.

(b) Add another polarizer and place it at the position z = 15. Use the Angle keyboard entry box to set the angle that the TA makes with the x-axis to be 90 degrees. What is the intensity of the light exiting this second polarizer? Verify that this is consistent with the way a linear polarizer works.

(c) Add another polarizer and position it at z = 10. Use the Angle keyboard entry box to set the angle that the TA makes with the x-axis to be 45 degrees. What is the polarization of the light exiting this polarizer? What is the polarization of the light exiting the last polarizer? Calculate the intensity of the light exiting the last polarizer and verify that its value agrees with WebTOP's result.

 

Exercise 6:  Half-Wave Plate – Linearly Polarized Light Incident

Choose the Polarized option for the incident electric field. From the pull-down menu on the left-hand side of the Console, choose Wave plate, and then click on the Add button.  Set the z value to 10, and the Thickness value to 0.50.

(a) Set the x component of the field to 0.866, the y-component to be 0.500, and the phase difference between the components to 0. The light incident upon the wave plate  is linearly polarized at 30 degrees to the positive x-axis. What is the polarization of the light exiting the wave plate? Verify that this is consistent with the way a half-wave plate works.

(b) Set the x component of the field to 0.500, the y-component to be 0.866, and the phase difference between the components to 0. The light incident upon the wave plate  is linearly polarized at 60 degrees to the positive x-axis. What is the polarization of the light exiting the wave plate? Verify that this is consistent with the way a half-wave plate works.

(c) Set the x component of the field to 0, the y-component to 1, and the phase difference between the components to be 0. The light incident upon the wave plate is linearly polarized in the y-direction. What is the polarization of the light exiting the wave plate? Why did the wave plate have no effect?

(d) Set the Angle value to 30 degrees, the x component of the field to 0.500, the y-component to be 0.866, and the phase difference between the components to 0. The light incident upon the wave plate  is linearly polarized at 60 degrees to the positive x-axis. What is the polarization of the light exiting the wave plate? Verify that this is consistent with the way a half-wave plate works.
