Polarization: Examples

To view an example, press the button at the end of the description. Once the module finishes loading, press the play button at the recording panel. If you want to save the example code to your computer and run it locally right click of the disk icon and save the file to disk. Use wsl as an extension of the file you save to your computer.

Example 1 Linearly Polarized Light

This example simulates the electric field of four types of linearly polarized, monochromatic light propagating in the positive z-direction. The cases depicted are, in order of their appearance, those for which the light is linearly polarized at the following angles with respect to the positive x-direction:
(a) 0 degrees,
(b) 45 degrees,
(c) 90 degrees, 
(d) -45 degrees.

Example 2  Linearly Polarized Light

This example simulates the electric field of five types of linearly polarized, monochromatic light propagating in the positive z-direction. The cases depicted are, in order of their appearance, those for which the light is linearly polarized at the following angles with respect to the positive x-direction:
(a) 0 degrees,
(b) 30 degrees,
(c) 45 degrees,
(d) 60 degrees,
(e) 90 degrees.

Example 3  Types of Polarization

This example simulates the electric field of four different types of polarization, in the following order:
(a) circularly polarized,
(b) elliptically polarized,
(c) linearly polarized, 
(d) unpolarized.

Example 6  Unpolarized Light

This example simulates the propagation the electric field of unpolarized light that is traveling in the positive z-direction.

Example 7  Linearly Polarized Light Incident Upon a Linear Polarizer

In this example a monochromatic electric field that is traveling in the positive z-direction and is linearly polarized at an angle of 45 degrees with respect to the positive x-direction is incident upon an ideal linear polarizer. The cases depicted are, in order of their appearance, those for which the transmission axis of the polarizer makes the following angles with the positive x-direction:
(a) 0 degrees,
(b) 45 degrees,
(c) 90 degrees, 
(d) 135 degrees (which equivalent to -45 degrees).

Example 8  Polarization by Absorption

In this example an unpolarized electric field that is traveling in the positive z-direction is incident upon an ideal linear polarizer. The cases depicted are, in order of their appearance, those for which the transmission axis makes the following angles with the positive x-direction:
(a) 0 degrees,
(b) 45 degrees,
(c) 90 degrees, 
(d) 135 degrees (which is equivalent to -45 degrees).

Example 11  Linearly Polarized and Circularly Polarized Light Incident Upon a Half-Wave Plate

This example simulates, consecutively, the following types of monochromatic electric fields incident upon a quarter-wave plate whose fast axis is parallel to the x-direction:
(a) light that is linearly polarized at 45 degrees with respect to the positive x-direction,
(b) left-circularly polarized light,
(c) light that is linearly polarized at -45 degrees with respect to the positive x-direction, 
(d) right-circularly polarized light.
