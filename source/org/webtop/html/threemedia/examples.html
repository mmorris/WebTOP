Reflection and Refraction - Waves Three Media: Examples

To view an example, press the button at the end of the description. Once the module finishes loading, press the play button at the recording panel. If you want to save the example code to your computer and run it locally right click of the disk icon and save the file to disk. Use wsl as an extension of the file you save to your computer.

Example 1  Frustrated Total Internal Reflection (FTIR)

This example begins by depicting FTIR with a fairly large thickness for medium 2. Initially the scene shows the resultant disturbance in the first media, but then the incident wave (only) is shown in the first media, and then the reflected wave (only). The second medium is then made thinner, and the process is repeated. Note that with the thinner second medium the amplitude of the wave transmitted into the third medium is larger than before.

Example 2  Antireflection Coating

This example depicts an "antireflection coating" situation: the thickness of the film (medium 2) is one-fourth of the wavelength of the light in the film, and the index of refraction of the film is the geometric mean of the other two media. The result is that the total reflected field in medium 1 is zero. This is illustrated by toggling between the following display options for the disturbance in medium 1: (a) incident field only (the initial situation), (b) reflected field only (note that its amplitude is zero), and (c) both (i.e. the superposition of the incident and reflected fields).
