Diffraction - Fresnel Single Slit: Examples

To view an example, press the button at the end of the description. Once the module finishes loading, press the play button at the recording panel. If you want to save the example code to your computer and run it locally right click of the disk icon and save the file to disk. Use wsl as an extension of the file you save to your computer.

Example 1 Changing the Width of the Slit: Even Fresnel Numbers

In this example a plane wave of light of wavelength 550 nm is normally incident upon a single slit, and the resulting intensity pattern is viewed on an observation screen 25 mm from the slit. In this simulation, the width of the slit is varied; slit widths that correspond to Fresnel numbers of 2, 4, 6, 8, and 10 are used, consecutively.  In each case, is the on-axis intensity a maximum or a minimum?  Starting at one edge of the intensity pattern and proceeding across it through the origin to the other edge, how many “major” maxima do you encounter?

Example 2  Changing the Wavelength of the Light: Even and Odd Fresnel Numbers

In this example a monochromatic plane wave of light is normally incident upon a single slit of width 0.75mm, and the resulting intensity pattern is viewed on an observation screen 50 mm from the slit. In this simulation, the wavelength of the incident light varies. Wavelengths that correspond to Fresnel numbers of 7, 6, 5 and 4 are used, consecutively.  In each case, is the on-axis intensity a maximum or a minimum?  Starting at one edge of the intensity pattern and proceeding across it through the origin to the other edge, how many “major” maxima do you encounter?

Example 3  Changing z, the Distance from the Aperture Plane to the Observation Plane: Odd Fresnel Numbers

In this example a plane wave of light of wavelength 550 nm is normally incident upon a single slit of width 0.75 mm, and the resulting intensity pattern is viewed on an observation screen. In this simulation, the distance z from the slit to the observation screen is varied.  Values of z that produce Fresnel numbers of 3,5,7,and 9 are used, consecutively. In each case, is the on-axis intensity a maximum or a minimum?  Starting at one edge of the intensity pattern and proceeding across it through the origin to the other edge, how many “major” maxima do you encounter?

Example 4  Transition from the Fresnel Region to the Fraunhofer Region

In this example a plane wave of light of wavelength 500 nm is normally incident upon a single slit of width 0.2 mm. The resulting intensity pattern is viewed on an observation screen a distance z from the slit . Six different values of z are used, in the following order: 10 mm, 20 mm, 30 mm, 40 mm, 50 mm, and 100 mm. In which case (or cases) is the observation screen in the Fraunhofer zone?
