Diffraction - Rayleigh Resolution Criterion: Examples

To view an example, press the button at the end of the description. Once the module finishes loading, press the play button at the recording panel. If you want to save the example code to your computer and run it locally right click of the disk icon and save the file to disk. Use wsl as an extension of the file you save to your computer.

Example 1 Resolution of Two Distant Sources: Changing Angular Separation of the Sources

In this example light from two 550 nm point sources passes through a lens of diameter 3.355 cm. The two images formed are viewed on an observation screen in the focal plane of the lens. Four different situations are depicted:
(a) the angular separation of the sources is 4.00 x 10-5 rad,
(b) the angular separation of the sources is 3.00 x 10-5 rad, 
(c) the angular separation of the sources is 2.00 x 10-5 rad, 
(d) the angular separation of the sources is 1.00 x 10-5 rad. 
In each case, look at the images on the screen and judge whether the two images are resolved or not. After doing this, check the angular separation against the Min Angle value in the lower right hand part of the Console. Min Angle is the smallest resolvable angular separation according to the Rayleigh criterion. 

Example 2 Resolution of Two Distant Sources: Changing the Wavelength of the Light

In this example light from two monochromatic point sources, whose angular separation is 2.00 x 10-5 rad, passes through a lens of diameter 3.355 cm. The two images formed are viewed on an observation screen in the focal plane of the lens. Four different situations are depicted:
(a) the wavelength of the sources is 400 nm,
(b) the wavelength of the sources is 500 nm, 
(c) the wavelength of the sources is 600 nm,
(d) the wavelength of the sources is 700 nm.
In each case, look at the images on the screen and judge whether the two images are resolved or not. After doing this, check the angular separation against the Min Angle value in the lower right hand part of the Console. Min Angle is the smallest resolvable angular separation according to the Rayleigh criterion. 

Example 3 Resolution of Two Distant Sources: Changing the Diameter of the Lens

In this example light from two 550 nm point sources whose angular separation is 2.00 x 10-5 rad passes through a lens. The two images formed are viewed on an observation screen in the focal plane of the lens. Three different situations are depicted:
(a) the diameter of the lens is 2.5 mm,
(b) the diameter of the lens of the sources is 3.0 mm,
(c) the diameter of the lens of the sources is 3.5 mm,
(d) the diameter of the lens is 4.0 cm, 
(e) the diameter of the lens is 4.5 mm.
