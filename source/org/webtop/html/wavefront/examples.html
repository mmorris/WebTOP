Wavefronts: Examples

To view an example, press the button at the end of the description. Once the module finishes loading, press the play button at the recording panel. If you want to save the example code to your computer and run it locally right click of the disk icon and save the file to disk. Use wsl as an extension of the file you save to your computer.

Example 1 Varying the Wavelength of a Spherical Wave

This example depicts the wavefronts associated with an harmonic spherical wave. The initial wavelength is 400 nm. The wavelength is then increased to 600 nm, and then to 800 nm. It is then decreased to 600 nm, and then to 400 nm. Note that as the wavelength increases (decreases), the distance between consecutive wavefronts increases (decreases).

 

Example 2  Varying the Wavelength of a Plane Wave

This example depicts the wavefronts associated with an harmonic plane wave. The initial wavelength is 400 nm. The wavelength is then increased to 600 nm, and then to 800 nm. It is then decreased to 600 nm, and then to 400 nm. Note that as the wavelength increases (decreases), the distance between consecutive wavefronts increases (decreases).

Example 3 Varying the Direction of Propagation of a Plane Wave

This example depicts the wavefronts associated with an harmonic plane wave. The wavevector, k, points in the direction of propagation of the waves. In this example the Rotate navigation icon is used to vary the direction of propagation of the plane wave.
