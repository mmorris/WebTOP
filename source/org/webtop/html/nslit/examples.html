Diffraction - Fraunhofer N-Slit: Examples

To view an example, press the button at the end of the description. Once the module finishes loading, press the play button at the recording panel. If you want to save the example code to your computer and run it locally right click of the disk icon and save the file to disk. Use wsl as an extension of the file you save to your computer.

Example 1  Single Slit – Effect of Changing the Slit Width or the Wavelength

In this example a plane wave of light of wavelength 500 nm is normally incident upon a single slit, and the resulting intensity pattern is displayed on an observation screen that is 1.00 meter from the slit. Three different situations are depicted as follows:
(a) The slit width starts at the value 0.10 mm, increases to 0.20 mm, and then increases to 0.30 mm.  
(b) The wavelength of the light increases from 500 nm to 600 nm, and then increases from 600 nm to 700 nm.  
(c) The slit width decreases to 0.20 mm, and then decreases to 0.10 mm.

Example 2  Two Narrow Slits – Effect of Changing the Distance Between the Slits or the Wavelength

In this example a plane wave of light of wavelength 500 nm is normally incident upon a two slits each of width 0.005 mm, and the resulting intensity pattern is displayed on an observation screen that is 1.00 meter from the slits. Three different situations are depicted as follows:
(a) The distance between the slits starts at the value 0.10 mm, increases to 0.15 mm, and then increases to 0.20 mm.  
(b) The wavelength of the light increases from 500 nm to 600 nm, and then increases from 600 nm to 700 nm.  
(c) The distance between the slits decreases to 0.15 mm, and then decreases to 0.10 mm.

Example 3  Double Slit - Effect of Changing the Slit Width

In this example a plane wave of light of wavelength 500 nm is normally incident upon two identical slits separated by center-to-center distance of 0.40 mm. The resulting intensity pattern is displayed on an observation screen 1.00 meter from the slits. Three different situations are depicted as follows:
(a) The slit width starts at the value 0.005 mm, increases to 0.050 mm, and then increases to 0.100 mm.
(b) The wavelength of the light is increases from 500 nm to 600 nm, and then increases from 600 nm to 700 nm.
(c) The number of slits toggled backs and forth between 1 and 2 to show that the single slit diffraction pattern modulates (in fact serves as the envelope for) the double slit interference pattern.

 

Example 4  Multiple Slits - Effect of Changing the Number of Slits

In this example a plane wave of light of wavelength 500 nm is normally incident upon a group of N slits that are each 0.005 mm wide. Consecutive slits are separated by a center-to-center distance of 0.10 mm. The resulting intensity pattern is displayed on an observation screen 1.00 meter from the slits. Five different situations are depicted as follows:
(a) N = 2,
(b) N = 3,
(c) N = 4
(d) N = 5,
(e) N = 10.

Example 5  Multiple Slits - Effect of Changing the Slit Separation or the Slit Width

In this example a plane wave of light of wavelength 500 nm is normally incident upon a group of five slits of width w separated by a center-to-center distance d. The resulting intensity pattern is displayed on an observation screen 1.00 meter from the slits. Two different situations are depicted as follows:
(a) With the slit width fixed at the value 0.005 mm, the slit separation starts at the value 0.10 mm, increases to 0.15 mm, and then to 0.20 mm.
(b) With the slit separation fixed at the value 0.20 mm, the slit width starts at the value 0.005 mm, increases to 0.01 mm, 0.03 mm, 0.05 mm, 0.07 mm, and finally to 0.1 mm.
